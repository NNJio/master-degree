Chickens

Origin: Chickens are domesticated from the red junglefowl of Southeast Asia. Scientific Name: Gallus gallus domesticus. Uses: Primarily raised for meat and eggs, but also kept as pets or for cockfighting. Global Significance: They are one of the most common and widespread domestic animals, with aF population exceeding 24 billion in 2003. Breeds: There are over 60 breeds of chickens.

Characteristics: Chickens are characterized by smaller heads, short beaks and wings, and a round body perched on featherless legs. Diet: They are omnivores, eating a variety of seeds, insects, and other small invertebrates. Life Cycle: Hatching: Fertile chicken eggs hatch after about 21 days of incubation. Chicks: Newly hatched chicks feed by absorbing theF internal yolk sac. Brooding: Hens remain on the nest for a few days after hatching, guarding and brooding their chicks to keep them warm. Imprinting: Chicks imprint on the hen and follow her continually.

Culinary Uses: Chicken meat is a versatile and popular protein source, used in countless dishes around the world. Nutritional Value: Chicken is a good source of protein, and it is lower in saturated fat than most meats, especially if the skin is removed.
